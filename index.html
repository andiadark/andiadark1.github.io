About Me:

  Andia Dark is a senior student at Florida Agricultural and Mechanical University studying Computer Science. She had the opportunity to be an Intel Scholar for the 2020-2021 school year. Along with this opportunity, she also enjoyed being an Information Technology Intern at Ford Motor Company for the summer of 2021. Andia is a driven student who hopes to earn the Summa Cum Laude honors upon graduation in the Spring of 2022. She plans to work for Ford Motors as an Information Technology FCG (Ford College Graduate) post-grad.

  Andia was born and raised in Brevard County, Florida. Andia is very close to her family, and she is the eldest of 4 girls. She enjoys going to the gym often to decompress and to gain strength. Also, as of January 1st, 2020, she has been practicing a vegan way of life to lessen her carbon footprint and reduce animal suffering because of animal agriculture. In the future, she hopes to open a vegan restaurant featuring some of her families' favorite recipes.

College Journey:

  Andia graduated high school in 2015 from Palm Bay Magnet Highschool. After graduation, she moved started college at the University of South Florida and majored in Electrical Engineering. After several semesters there, she realized Electrical Engineering was not for her. Fall of 2018, she transferred to Hillsborough Community College and declared her major to be Computer Science. After a year of classes, she graduated with her AA in Computer Science and transferred to Florida Agricultural and America. She is currently on track to graduate with her bachelor's degree in the Spring of 2022.

Why Computer Science?

  She loves problem-solving, probably a little too much. Andia is the type of person to keep trying to solve a problem until it's solved. Programming keeps you on your toes. Programming itself is not that hard, but finding efficient solutions to problems can be a challenge, but that is what makes it fun!
